## Problem 1 Short Response Questions (20 pts)


1. Show that in vacuum, if the electric field vectors at all points point in the same direction, then the magnitude of the electric field must be uniform.

2. Prove that the vector field $\frac{\vec r}{r^3}$ is divergence-free and curl-free everywhere except at the origin. 
   You may either provide a direct vector-calculus proof or argue using known properties of electrostatic fields.

3. Faraday cage: similar to the phenomenon that signals get significantly weaker in elevators.
   
   (i) Explain why a person inside a vehicle usually remains safe when the car is struck by lightning during a thunderstorm.
   
   (ii) If a charged object is placed inside a Faraday cage, will it produce any effect outside the cage? Explain.

4. Two infinite parallel conducting plates are placed facing each other. Label the four surfaces, from left to right, as $A, B, C, D$, where $A$ and $B$ are the left and right surface of the first (left) metal plate, and $C$ and $D$ are the left and right surface of the second (right) metal plate. Prove that:

    (i)(2 pts) $\sigma_B + \sigma_C = 0$
   
    (ii)(3 pts) $\sigma_A = \sigma_D$, where $\sigma$ denotes the surface charge density.

## Problem 3 (10 pts)
**(1)** Consider an infinitely long uniformly charged straight line with linear charge density $\lambda$. Determine the resulting electric field distribution.

**(2)** Consider a semi-infinite uniformly charged ray with linear charge density $\lambda$, whose endpoint is $P$. Let point $O$ satisfy that $OP$ is perpendicular to the ray and $|OP| = R$. Find the electric field at point $P$.

**Hint:** Consider using angle integrals.

**(3)** Consider a charged circular arc of radius $R$ and central angle $\alpha$ where $\alpha=\frac{\pi}{2}$ and $\alpha=\pi$ in this case. From each endpoint of the arc, a semi-infinite charged ray (with the same linear charge density $\lambda$) extends tangentially from the circle. For both cases, determine the electric field at the center of the circle.

## Problem 4 (10 pts)
Consider a three-dimensional space where the region $z < 0$ is filled with a linear dielectric material of relative permittivity $\varepsilon_r$. An infinite straight line charge, carrying a uniform linear charge density $\lambda$, is placed parallel to the $x$-axis and passes through the point $(0, 0, h)$, where $h > 0$.

**(1)** Determine where the polarization charges are distributed within the system.

**(2)** Considering the boundary condition  $D_{1n} = D_{2n}$ on the $xOy$-plane, find the force per unit length acting on the line charge.

## Problem 5 (10 pts)
A coaxial cylindrical capacitor is formed by two conducting cylindrical shells whose common axis lies along the $y$-axis. The inner conductor has an inner radius $R_1$, and the outer conductor has an inner radius $R_2$. The space between the two conductors is filled with two linear dielectric materials of permittivities $\varepsilon_1$ and $\varepsilon_2$, which occupy the upper and lower halves, respectively, of the region between the cylinders. The outer conductor is grounded, and the inner conductor carries a (free) charge per unit length of $\lambda$. 
Find:  

**(1)** The surface free charge distribution on the inner conductor.  

**(2)** The polarization charge distribution (both on the inner conductor surface and at the interface between the two dielectric regions). Determine whether there exists any polarization charge at the interface of the two dielectrics.  

**(3)** The capacitance per unit length of the system and the energy density per unit length.

## Problem 6 (10 pts)
**(1)** Consider a sphere of radius $R$ made of a linear dielectric material with permittivity $\varepsilon$. Inside the sphere, the polarization field is given by $\vec{P} = K \frac{\vec{r}}{r^2}$, where $K$ is a constant and $\vec{r}$ is the position vector. Determine the distribution of the polarization charge density both inside the sphere and on its surface.

**(2)** Find the distribution of free charge density within the dielectric sphere. There are no free charge density on the surface of the dielectric sphere.

**(3)** Determine the electric field and the electric potential distribution both inside and outside the sphere.

**(4)** Calculate the electrostatic energy stored in the entire system.

## Problem 7 (15 pts)
**(1)** Consider two infinitely long lines with uniform linear charge distributions, located at $y = d$ and $y = -d$, carrying charge densities $\lambda$ and $-\lambda$ per unit length, respectively. Prove that the equipotential surfaces in space are cylindrical surfaces.

**(2)** Consider a cylindrical conducting shell of radius $R$, whose axis coincides with the $y$-axis, and whose total charge is zero. A line of uniform charge density $\lambda$ is parallel to the axis of the cylinder, located at a distance $l$ from the axis ($l>R$), and lies in the $xOy$-plane. Find the surface charge density $\sigma(\theta)$ at a point on the conducting shell that makes an angle $\theta$ with the $xOy$-plane.

**Hint:** for this question, use the result in question (1).

## Problem 8 (15 pts)
**(1)** A spherical surface of radius $R$, centered at the origin, carries a surface charge density given by $\sigma(\theta) = \sigma_0 \cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is the polar angle measured from the $x$-axis. Determine the electric field distribution both inside and outside the sphere. 

**Hint:** Consider the superposition of two uniformly charged spheres of radius $R$ with equal and opposite total charges, whose centers are slightly displaced along the $x$-axis.

**(2)** A grounded conducting spherical shell has an inner radius $R$, with its center at the origin. A point electric dipole $\vec{p}$, oriented along the $x$-axis, is placed at the origin. Find the electric field distribution within the cavity of the shell, and determine the induced surface charge distribution on the inner surface.

**(3)** A dielectric sphere of radius $R$ and relative permittivity $\varepsilon_r$ is placed in a uniform external electric field of magnitude $E_0$, directed along the $x$-axis. Find the electric field distribution everywhere in space and the polarization charge distribution on the surface of the sphere.
